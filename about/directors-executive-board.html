Directors & Executive Board

Directors & Executive Board

James H. McKerrow, MD, PhD, Founder and CEO

James H. McKerrow, MD, PhD, past Director of the Center for Discovery & Innovation in Parasitic Diseases (CDIPD) at UCSF, Professor in the Schools of Medicine and Pharmacy, and Vice-Chair of the Department of Pathology at UCSF. An authority on the biology of parasitic organisms and anti-parasite chemotherapy, he is author of 250 peer-reviewed publications and holds 5 US patents. More information at http://pathology.ucsf.edu/mckerrow/James_McKerrow.html

Roger Linington, PhD, Chief Scientific Officer

Roger Linington, PhD, Assistant Professor of Chemistry at the University of California, Santa Cruz, where he leads a multi-disciplinary group focused on the discovery, isolation and synthetic modification of natural products, with a particular focus on marine-derived bacterial sources. More information at http://www.chemistry.ucsc.edu/faculty/linington.html

Larissa M. Podust, PhD, VP for Research & Development

Protein crystallographer, Director of the Structural Biology Core at CDIPD,  and Adjunct Professor of Pathology at UCSF, she is an authority on the interactions of natural products with the P450 reductase system and the role these enzymes play in diseases like Chagas disease and tuberculosis. She is author of 50 peer-reviewed publications. More information at http://podustlab.org

Mera Granberg, Director of Strategic Development

Founding executive director of the San Francisco General Hospital Foundation, led a national sales program for Endymion Systems, and has led communications and fund raising efforts for local and national political campaigns.

Potter Wickware, MS, Communications Officer and Secretary-Treasurer

Science writer at UCSF’s CDIPD and Editor in the Department of Pathology at UCSF. He is a book author, past publisher of the newsletter Biotech Brief, has written news and feature stories for the Macmillan Nature publications, the New York Times and many other publications. More information at http://pwickware.com

Michael Fischbach, PhD, Consultant

Assistant Professor in the Department of Bioengineering and Therapeutic Sciences at UCSF and a member of the California Institute of Quantitative Biosciences (QB3). Fischbach is a recipient of the NIH Director's New Innovator Award, a Fellowship for Science and Engineering from the David and Lucille Packard Foundation, a Medical Research Award from the W.M. Keck Foundation, and the Young Investigator Grant for Probiotics Research from the Global Probiotics Council. His laboratory uses a combination of genomics and chemistry to identify and characterize small molecules from microbes, with an emphasis on the human microbiome. Fischbach received his Ph.D. in chemistry from Harvard in 2007, where he studied the role of iron acquisition in bacterial pathogenesis and the biosynthesis of antibiotics. Before coming to UCSF, he spent two years as an independent fellow at Massachusetts General Hospital coordinating a collaborative effort based at the Broad Institute to develop genomics-based approaches to the discovery of small molecules from microbes. More information at http://www.fischbachgroup.org
