Neglected Tropical Diseases

What are neglected tropical diseases?

A group of mainly parasitic infections that collectively afflict at least half the worlds population, NTDs are largely overlooked by the pharmaceutical industry because of adverse economic incentives. Existing therapies have drawbacks of toxicity, high cost or lack of efficacy due to drug resistance. A partial list:

  • Chagas disease is the leading cause of heart disease in Central America. The 2 available drugs, nifurtimox and benznidazole, are contra-indicated for pregnancy and carry the danger of hepatic or renal failure. Side effects of nifurtimox may include psychiatric and neurological disorders and seizures. No new drugs have been developed for Chagas for 40 years.
  • The filariases, a group of permanently disabling diseases caused by parasitic worms endemic in sub-Saharan Africa and the Pacific Basin, cause blindness and grotesque malformations of the lower extremities. Ivermectin, a well-tolerated oral drug, kills only immature worms but does not eradicate infection. Diethylcarbamazine kills both adult and juvenile worms but carries the risk of fatal brain inflammation. Albendazole, an effective broad-spectrum drug, can cause hair loss and other side effects including jaundice and anemia.
  • Malaria, the fifth leading cause of death worldwide, mainly afflicts children under the age of 5. A child in Africa typically has 1-5 malaria infections a year; every 30 seconds a child dies from this disease. The natural products quinine and artemisinin and their derivatives are available, but drug resistance is a growing threat.
  • Leishmaniasis, endemic in parts of Asia, Africa, Latin America and the Mediterranean region, presents cutaneously with often disfiguring ulcers and tissue degeneration, or in a visceral form which can be fatal if untreated. First-line treatments based on the metalloid element antimony have been in use for more than 70 years. Growing resistance, especially in India, renders the antimonials ineffective. Amphotericin B, a replacement therapy, has severe and potentially lethal side effects.
  • Schistosomiasis, a group of debilitating dirty water diseases caused by parasitic worms and spread by snails, afflicts more than 200 million people in Africa, the Middle East, parts of the Caribbean, South America and the Pacific Basin. Praziquantel, the only available treatment, has been in use since the mid-70s and resistance is a growing threat.
  • African Sleeping Sickness, a disease of the central nervous system, is endemic in 36 countries in sub-Saharan Africa. It causes irreversible neurological damage, coma and death. Animal forms of the disease severely impact agriculture and local economies. Drugs like Suramin, pentamidine and melarsoprol (a mixture of arsenic and propylene glycol, or antifreeze) have harsh side effects and may themselves cause mortality.
